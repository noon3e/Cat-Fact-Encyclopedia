Cat Facts

This section is for all your random cat fact needs. Feel free to explore them, & this section full of my amazon recommendations if you're interested in viewing ones like them.

Domesticated cats still practice things that their cousins, Tigers whom they share 95.6% of their DNA with in the wild. As a result,

  • Cats bury their feces because in the wild, they can tell each other’s feces apart. It’s a natural response as they are ambush predators, & don’t want other animals, or larger predators to be able to track their scent easily.
  • Cats sleep about 70% of their life, compared to humans who sleep about 30-40%. Cats are apex predators, & predators sleep to conserve their energy in the wild.
  • Cats spend about 50% of their waking life grooming themselves, & about 70% of their life asleep, compared to humans who sleep about 30-40%.
  • Cats are apex predators, & predators sleep to conserve their energy in the wild.
  • Overtime cats have evolved to learn that stagnant water sources are more likely to make them sick, and as a result prefer moving water sources. As such, click here to view the section that has recommendations for water fountains to prevent this.
  • Perpetually dehydrated cats are highly-susceptible to kidney, & Urinary Tract Infections. UTI's can also make your fur baby throw up.
  • Food = Love to cats. With that said, to them, when you feed them that means you love them.
  • Cats sometimes go belly up to say hello, & to signify they trust you. If your feline friend does not like their belly rubbed but shows you their belly when they see you, it’s not because they want their belly rubbed, but rather that they trust you, & are comfortable around you.
  • Cats scratch to spread their scent and as a stress reliever.
  • Cats also lick things as a stress reliever, which is why (I personally consider) Lickable Treats to be a necessary part of a cat’s diet. Lickable treats are also a wonderful way to bond with a pet.
  • Cats like their towers & furniture moved around their living space.
  • Adult Cats require less food than kittens.
  • Cats are highly trainable, including learning to associate words with buttons. As thus, communicate with their humans.
    Watch this youtube video of Billi the Cat using buttons to asking his pawrent why they didn't respond faster than they did.
  • Unfortantely, cats cannot taste sweet things.
  • Cats can see red green and blue and combinations thereof.
  • There around 73-80 recognized cat breeds across associations, all with their own number of “official” cat breeds.
  • Although cats cannot understand fluent english, they still value a good conversation. As a result, slowly learn words through associations, kind of like the talking pet buttons, just without the physical queue being a button.
  • Cats can make up to 100 sounds, in comparison to dogs who can only make around 10.
  • Cats meow to communicate with humans, but rarely vocalize to communicate with other cats.
  • Male cats are more likely to be left-pawed, while females right-pawed.
  • The vibrations from a cat purring resonating at 20-150 Hertz have been proven to be therapeutic. They use it to heal themselves, as much as bone-regeneration, or us when we are sick or injured.
  • Cats can recognize their owner by their voice.
  • Cats can jump up to 6 times their length.
  • A cats sense of smell is 14 times stronger than a human’s.
  • The average lifespan of a cat has risen from about 7 years in the early 1980s, to 15 years by 2021.
  • Most black cats have yellow eyes.
  • When a cat blinks at you it is their way of showing affection. Feel absolutely free to blink back to reciprocate the sweet gesture.
  • A happy cat “makes biscuits” or kneads on an object of choice, or in the air.
  • If your feline friend is a young kitten, it is highly recommended not to use clumping litter. Kittens like babies learn by putting things in their mouths. Clumping litter can cause “rock-like” obstructions.
  • A single intact female can produce up to 40,000 surviving offspring in a span of 10 years.
  • Cats don’t rely on us for social structure. They see us as “fellow cats". For this reason they may bring you gifts, kills from around the house, or their favorite toys.
New Adopt Advice
    You may need:
  • Food/Water Bowls or Automatic Feeders
  • A water fountain
  • “Scent Soakers”
  • Kitten Food
  • Wet Food
  • A Litter Box
  • Non-clumping Litter
  • A pet carrier
  • Scratching Posts or Boards
  • A "hidey hole"
  • Toys & Enrichment
  • Fish Oil, (and other) Supplements
  • Pet Nail Clippers
  • A Cat Tower
  • A Pet Brush

Felines prefer drinking from moving water sources. If you can afford it, investing in a water fountain can entice your kitty to drink more often. Keeping them well hydrated, & free of kidney stones or UTI's. I noticed my kitten's water intake drastically increased after switching to an automatic water fountain. See this section full of my amazon recommendations to get you started with some ideas.

Non-porous material is the most ideal for food & water bowls, same goes for automatic feeders & fountains. Plastic is porous & holds germs really well. This causes chin acne in some cats. Stainless Steel, Pyrex, Glass or Ceramic bowls are wonderful alternatives to plastic.

A food bowl or automatic feeder. If feeding your kitten 4 times a day for about 365 days seems like a big ask, or obligations such as work &/or personal life would interfere with frequent feedings, I recommend investing in a high quality automatic feeder. You will appreciate all of the time and energy you get back as a result.

Tiki Baby or Fancy Feast kitten pate kept my kitten happy & content.

Although not completely necessary, fish oil aids in skin, fur, and brain development for a growing kitten. I notice the way my kitten’s coat shines since supplementing fish oil. He also really enjoys the taste of it.

For picky eaters, Nutritional Yeast, & Meal Toppers as mealtime mix-ins Fortified Nutritional Yeast is an ingredient commonly found in a plethora of cat treats and sometimes food. It is a flavor enhancer as it tastes sort of cheesy. However, while tasty, it is very good for your kittens health. It contains B vitamins, and (other info) Meal toppers or meal mixers are typically liquid based, & add flavor to your kittens food while softening it as well.

I fed my kitten Purina One Plus Kitten Food, but personally would have gone with Tiki Baby if I had a choice, & could do it again.

Some kind of cat hideout to retreat to until they are comfortable in your space. I personally recommend putting this “hidey hole” in the room you dedicate to them, and slowly moving it to a different area in the house. As they begin exploring your space, this will make them feel more confident as if they get spooked, they have a safe space to retreat to.

A room you can dedicate to them, most people choose the bathroom, that's what I went with.

A litter box. Cats, young and old are predisposed to using “litter like” things. So if you find yourself victim to the cat distrubtion system after a late night McDonald's run, something that is "litter-like" will suffice until stores can open in the morning. Plastic boxes, old amazon box deliveries, (lined of course.) ect. will work until you can find an open store.

Toys and Enrichment see this section on toys and enrichment to see my personal recommendations on toys. I do, however in the beginning recommend getting “one of each kind,” until you find the type of toy (or toys) your kitten has an affinity for. This will save you lots of time and money, guaranteed. The easiest way to do this is buying a variety pack, and slowly introducing the toys to your kitten, or new adopt. You will quickly see what they gravitate to. I have a Bombay cat, which are one of the most highly active breeds. Although he will play with almost anything, he still has a few types of toys he gravitates to.

I recommend once the investment in a carrier has been made, to leave it out so your cat assumes it's a normal part of the day to day. Cats tend to fight getting into their carrier because it does not feel like their choice. I even went as far to incorporate his carrier into playtime, & even throwing treats in it, thus creating positive associations. This made it so he would jump into his carrier on his own when it was time to go on some occasions.

Some cats have problems with proper hydration. This can be fixed by mixing a tiny bit of water into their wet food, or some kind of liquid based meal topper, or squeezeable treat. I recommend delectable stew packs, or Tiki Cat squeezeable treats.

Kitten proof your home until they learn the rules. Kittens & new adopts are babies, they learn like we do by making mistakes. If you care about it, kitten proof it.

Scratching posts. Cats soothe themselves, & mark their scent by scratching things. Teaching them young & early where scratching is allowed will keep your couches in pristine shape for longer.

Brush your cats teeth with feline friendly toothpaste. Brushing with human-grade toothpaste can make them seriously ill or worse, it can be fatal.

Cut your indoor cats nails regularly. I shoot for once a week to biweekly. If your cats nails get too long, they can curl into their paw pad, causing them serious pain, and you an expensive vet bill.

Catify your home! It absolutely does not need to happen all at once. But slowly add nice things that your cat loves to your space. For us in the beginning, I would buy little things biweekly to add to his collection. This will make adjusting to their new space much easier on them, thus on you the human.

Scent soakers. Soft things like blankets and beds for them to knead in and make them feel & smell like it is their's.

There are natural dewormers that can be added to food or water so you don’t have to traumatize them by “pilling” them. My cat hates pilling, the natural option is a wonderful backup & also much cheaper. Click here for my personal recommendation for herbal deworming.

Cats are obligate carnivores, as such need to eat dry food that is at least 26 to 30 percent protein, to guarantee healthy growth, development, and (reproduction if you are into breeding.)

Can My Cat Eat That?

This section will be a list composed of things that are & aren't recommended to be fed to your special kitty.

Yes you can give your cat the following :

  • L-Lysine Supplements
  • Cooked Eggs
  • Silvervine Powder
  • Kefir
  • Chamomile
  • Valerian Root : Has a similar affect to catnip or silvervine powder. Can be used in place of catnip if your cat does not respond to catnip.
  • Cosequin Treats
  • Pumpkin puree to assist with digestion &/or vomiting or stomach upset.
  • Fish Oil
  • Vitamin E Supplements
  • Nutritional Yeast
  • Bone Broth
  • Organic Coconut Oil

No you cannot give your feline friend the following :

  • Chocolate
  • Raw Eggs
  • Raw Meat
  • Raw Fish : raw fish contains enzymes that breakdown vitamin b1 in a cat's body resulting in a deficiency. This deficiency can lead to neurological problems, seizures & locomotion disorders, and it can be fatal for your cat.
  • Lilies
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Aloe Vera
  • Grapes, Currants & Raisins : Grapes contain a compound that has yet to be identified, which affects the kidneys & can lead to sudden renal failure.
  • Raw Yeast Dough : Raw dough will rise while it is the stomach of a cat. This can cause a lot of pain & actually stretch their stomach. As it sits, the alcohol ferments, which produces alcohol. This can result alcohol poisoning.
  • Dairy products : Cats cannot process dairy products, most cats lake or only have small amounts of the enzyme lactase, which gives the body the ability to process and breakdown the enzyme.
  • Xylitol : Xylitol is an artificial sweetener used in many human foods and other products, including chewing gum, sweets, nut butters, bakery goods, diabetic foods and toothpaste. If ingested, can cause an abnormal increase in insulin release from the pancreas, which in turn leads to a decrease in overall blood sugar levels.
  • Mushrooms : Mushrooms contain compounds that are toxic to cats. These toxins can affect multiple different systems in the body and even result in death.
